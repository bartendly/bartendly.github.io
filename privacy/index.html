For data protection purposes, Bartendly OÜ is the data controller for personal data processed through Bartendly, Bartendly Experiences, our websites, forms, communications, and related services.

2. Services covered by this policy

This Privacy Policy applies when you interact with:

  • Bartendly: the app, platform, directory, event discovery tools, professional profiles, and related digital services.
  • Bartendly Experiences: our commercial bartending service coordination activity for private customers, corporate clients, agencies, exhibitions, brand activations, and events.
  • Website and communications: our public website, contact forms, WhatsApp links, emails, social media messages, and related enquiries.

3. Personal data we collect

The personal data we collect depends on how you interact with Bartendly.

  • Clients and event enquiries: name, email address, phone number, WhatsApp number, company name, event type, event date, location, venue, guest count, service requirements, budget information if provided, and any other details you send to us.
  • Corporate and agency clients: business contact details, company information, billing details, event information, communications, contract details, invoices, and payment status.
  • Bartenders and hospitality professionals: name, city, country, professional title, work experience, profile photos, biography, languages, skills, availability, Instagram or social links, email, WhatsApp, and information you choose to include in your profile or communications with us.
  • Website and app users: basic technical information such as device type, browser type, approximate location, pages viewed, app activity, log data, and cookie or analytics information where applicable.
  • Communications: emails, WhatsApp messages, form submissions, social media messages, support requests, feedback, and other correspondence with Bartendly.

4. How we use personal data

We use personal data to operate Bartendly and Bartendly Experiences, including to:

  • reply to event requests and general enquiries;
  • prepare quotes, proposals, booking confirmations, contracts, and invoices;
  • coordinate bartending services, staffing, availability, schedules, and operational details;
  • connect confirmed clients with assigned bartenders where necessary for a booking;
  • create, display, manage, and update bartender or professional profiles where applicable;
  • publish or manage event, bar, brand, or hospitality-related information submitted to Bartendly;
  • communicate with bartenders about gigs, opportunities, profile visibility, platform updates, and important service information;
  • communicate with clients about enquiries, bookings, service changes, payment, cancellation, and support;
  • improve our website, app, services, user experience, and commercial processes;
  • prevent fraud, spam, misuse, unsafe requests, or unauthorized access;
  • comply with legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, or dispute-resolution obligations.

5. Legal bases for processing

We process personal data only where we have a legal basis to do so. Depending on the situation, we rely on:

  • Contract or pre-contractual steps: when we process data to respond to a request, prepare a quote, coordinate a booking, manage a profile, or provide a service.
  • Legitimate interests: when we operate, secure, improve, and promote Bartendly, manage professional relationships, prevent misuse, and maintain business records, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Consent: when you voluntarily submit certain profile information, agree to appear publicly, subscribe to optional communications, or where consent is required by law.
  • Legal obligations: when we need to keep accounting records, invoices, tax documents, or comply with applicable laws and lawful requests.

6. Public profiles and visibility

If you join or appear on Bartendly as a bartender, bar, brand, event organizer, or hospitality professional, some information may be displayed publicly if it was provided or approved for that purpose. This may include your name, professional title, city, profile photo, biography, experience, social links, associated bars, events, travel plans, or other professional information.

Private contact details such as your email address and WhatsApp number are not published unless you choose to make them public or explicitly ask us to display them. We may share private contact details with confirmed clients, assigned professionals, or operational partners only when reasonably necessary for a real booking, event, collaboration, or support request.

7. Sharing personal data

We do not sell personal data. We share personal data only where necessary for the purposes described in this Policy.

  • Assigned bartenders or professionals: for confirmed or serious event coordination, only the information needed to assess or perform the service.
  • Clients: limited professional information about assigned bartenders or service providers where needed for a booking.
  • Service providers: hosting, infrastructure, email, forms, analytics, payment, accounting, communication, security, and operational tools used to run Bartendly.
  • Communication platforms: if you contact us through WhatsApp, Instagram, email, or other third-party platforms, your use of those platforms is also subject to their own privacy terms.
  • Legal or safety reasons: where disclosure is required by law, necessary to protect rights, safety, security, or to handle disputes, fraud, misuse, or legal claims.
  • Business changes: if Bartendly is involved in a merger, restructuring, investment, sale, or transfer of business assets, personal data may be shared where reasonably necessary and subject to appropriate safeguards.

8. International data transfers

Bartendly is operated from Estonia but may support clients, bartenders, events, and service providers in different countries. Your personal data may therefore be processed in countries outside your country of residence, including outside the European Economic Area.

Where required, we use appropriate safeguards for international transfers, such as contractual protections, standard contractual clauses, adequacy decisions, or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

9. Data retention

We keep personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.

  • Event enquiries and leads: kept for a reasonable period to manage follow-up, commercial history, and future related requests.
  • Confirmed bookings, contracts, invoices, and payment records: kept for the period required for accounting, tax, legal, and dispute-resolution purposes.
  • Bartender and professional profiles: kept while the profile is active or while there is a legitimate reason to maintain the information, unless deletion is requested and no legal reason requires retention.
  • Communications: kept as needed to manage requests, bookings, disputes, safety, support, and business records.
  • Technical and analytics data: kept for a limited period appropriate to security, analytics, and service-improvement purposes.

10. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure. Access to personal data is limited to people and service providers who need it for the purposes described in this Policy.

No website, app, email, or online communication method is completely secure. If you believe your data or communication with Bartendly may have been compromised, please contact us at contact@bartendly.com.

11. Cookies and analytics

Bartendly aims to use cookies and similar technologies in a limited and practical way. Some cookies or local storage may be necessary for website or app operation, security, preferences, or basic functionality.

We may use basic analytics to understand how visitors use our website or app and to improve the service. We do not sell personal data and do not aim to track users across the internet for advertising purposes. If we introduce non-essential analytics or marketing cookies that require consent, we will provide the appropriate notice or consent mechanism.

12. Your rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • request access to personal data we hold about you;
  • request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data;
  • request deletion of personal data where applicable;
  • request restriction of processing;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing;
  • request portability of personal data where applicable;
  • withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
  • lodge a complaint with a data protection authority.

To exercise your rights, contact us at contact@bartendly.com. We may need to verify your identity before responding to certain requests.

13. Marketing communications

We may contact clients, bartenders, bars, brands, agencies, or hospitality professionals about relevant Bartendly services, opportunities, updates, or collaborations where permitted by law. You can ask us to stop sending non-essential marketing communications at any time by contacting contact@bartendly.com.

14. Children

Bartendly is not intended for children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data to Bartendly, please contact us so we can review and delete it where appropriate.

15. Automated decision-making

Bartendly does not use automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects on individuals. We may use software tools to help organize requests, profiles, event data, or communications, but important commercial and operational decisions are reviewed by people.

16. Updates to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our services, legal requirements, or business operations. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised update date.

This Privacy Policy is intended to be clear and practical for users, clients, bartenders, and partners. It does not replace any mandatory rights you may have under applicable data protection law.
